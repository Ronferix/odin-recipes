Salmon Poke Bowl

Description

This poke is easy to make! The recipe is very flexible,
so you'll be able to use your favorite toppings to enjoy
and custom this bowl.

Cheaper to do than to buy in a restaurant!
And made for you, by you!

Ingridients

Spring Rolls

  • 4 cups of cooked rice
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 4 teaspoons of rice wine vinegar
  • 4 teaspoons of sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoons of chile oil
  • 12 ounces sashimi-grade salmon, cut into small cubes

Toppings

  • 1 avocado, or to taste, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, or to taste, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of shelled edamame, or to taste
  • 4 sheets of dried seaweed, or to taste, cut into strips
  • 1 tablespoon of pickled ginger, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of furikake (Japanese nori seasoning), or to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped green onion, or to taste
  • 1 teblespoon of sesame seeds, or to taste

Steps

  1. Divide rice among four bowls.
  2. Combine soy sauce, rice wine vinegar, sesame oil, and chile oil in a bowl; add salmon and toss to coat. Divide among the four bowls.
  3. Top bowls with avocado, bell pepper, edamame, seaweed, pickled ginger, and furikake seasoning. Garnish with chopped green onion; sprinkle with sesame seeds.
